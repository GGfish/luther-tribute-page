Martin Luther

The Man Who Changed Everything

Bronze statue of Martin Luther holding the German translated Bible, outside of the Church of our Lady in Desden, Germany. Crafted by Adolf von Donndorf.

Milestones of Martin's life:

  • 1483 - born in Eisleben, Germany
  • 1498 - start's his education in Eisenach, Germany.
  • 1501 - to please his father, goes to study canon law at the University of Erfurt.
  • 1505 - gets caught in a terrible lightning storm. Out of fear, he vows to St. Anne that if she saves him from the storm, he would become a monk. After surviving the storm, he drops his career as a lawyer, and join's the Augustinian order at the monastery in Erfurt.
  • 1508 - after completing his first mass, he is sent to Wittenberg to study and lecture at the local university.
  • 1510 - walks 1000 miles to Rome.
  • 1517 - preaches against the selling of indulgences. On Oct 31st, he nails the 95 Thesis to the door of Wittenberg's Castle Church. The news spreads like wildfire.
  • 1518 - charged with heresy in Rome. He defends himself in Augsburg with argument's based soley on the Bible rather than church doctrine. He is forced to flee back to Wittenburg, and sheltered by Fredrick the Wise.
  • 1520 - burns the Papal Bull in Wittenberg.
  • 1521 - excommunicated and summoned to appear before the Council of Worms. Presured by the council to recant his views, he stated, "I neither can nor will recant". Upon stating this he changed the course of history. On his return to Wittenberg, he was kidnapped and taken to Wartburg Castle. His kidnappers were sent by his friend, Frederick the Wise, in order to hide him from the Papacy who wanted him dead. He temporarily takes on the alias of "Squire George". From there he translated the New Testament into German for the common people.
  • 1522 - returns to Wittenberg to begin a series of both theological and social reforms. One being the education of all common people, and the freedom for musicians to write their own hymns for church services.
  • 1525 - marries Katharina von Bora, a former nun.
  • 1537 - Schmalkalden hosts the a meeting of the Schmalkaldic League of rulers and theologians. There he presents his Article of Faith, which introduced the Lutheran doctrine.
  • 1546 - dies in Eisleben. His coffin is brought back to Wittenburg and buried at the Castle Church. He lived for 63 years, 2 months, and 10 days.
